The hardware costs for V1G (smart charging) and V2G (bidirectional) technology represent a significant disparity in the capital investment required for fleet electrification.

### **Direct Hardware Cost Comparison**
According to the sources, the price difference for the charging units alone is substantial:
*   **V1G (Smart Charging):** Standard smart chargers cost between **$5,000 and $15,000** per unit.
*   **V2G (Bidirectional):** Bidirectional inverters are significantly more expensive, ranging from **$40,000 to over $60,000** per unit.

### **Economic and Strategic Context**
The AXIVAI platform prioritises a **"V1G-First" strategy** because of this cost-to-value ratio. While V2G hardware offers the theoretical ability to provide grid services and generate revenue from the utility, it is currently hampered by **immature hardware** and **certification challenges**. 

In contrast, V1G hardware is:
*   **Fully Approved:** V1G systems face none of the regulatory hurdles currently delaying V2G deployments.
*   **High Value Capture:** Despite the lower hardware cost, V1G is designed to capture the majority of a fleet's immediate economic value through **demand charge avoidance** and the use of **off-peak rates**.
*   **Warranty Safe:** Standard V1G chargers maintain standard OEM battery coverage, whereas V2G hardware can lead to potential concerns regarding battery warranties due to increased cycling.

As noted in our conversation history, V1G effectively provides roughly **80% of the economic benefit** of energy management at approximately **20% of the cost and complexity** associated with V2G systems. This makes V1G the pragmatic choice for districts looking to deploy quickly without the financial and regulatory risks of bidirectional technology.
